# Specialists - Discovery & Worker Guidance

A specialist owns one hypothesis class. The coordinator discovers sibling specialist skills from their frontmatter `name` and `description`, then dispatches selected specialists as subagents.

Honesty floor: [../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md).

Operational efficiency floor: [../operational-discipline.md](../operational-discipline.md). Probes are gap-closing (name the claim/discriminator each closes); "sufficient" = the live discriminator is resolved. Operate under both floors; honesty wins.

## Discovery

This file is not a registry. A specialist description should be enough for routing: identify the hypothesis class, include trigger terms or obligation themes, and state that it operates within an investigation workflow that supplies its context, not as a direct user entrypoint.

The coordinator routes from descriptions; it does not load specialist bodies for routing, and children do not load the coordinator skill.

## Worker guidance

Use [../run-store.md](../run-store.md), [../artifact-contracts.md](../artifact-contracts.md), and [../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md) as needed. Keep the local role simple:

- Run as a subagent. Do **not** load the coordinator skill.
- Own your assigned hypothesis class and write only under `4_specialists/<your-name>/`.
- Start from the coordinator's brief: your hypothesis area, output directory, the skills you were passed, and any existing observations. Assign new `OBS###` ids as you record observations so the report can cite an unbroken chain.
- Worker class follows the coordinator's Dispatch routing (evidence-using specialists run `full-evidence`).
- Fetch and analyze your own observations through the passed skills; reuse existing observation ids when they already answer the question.
- Cite observation ids for material claims.
- Name cause plus mechanism in any theory you advance.
- Pre-register before you check: take the pre-registered discriminator for your hypothesis from Scout's discriminator table, and WRITE the predicate plus the expected favored vs rival observation BEFORE you query the value. You may refine Scout's discriminator only from mechanism understanding, source/schema shape, or authoritative-source fit — NOT from the observed discriminator value or a symptom-correlated result; if any observed result shaped the predicate or the expected favored/rival values, it is no longer pre-registered. Then check it and record the observed value and whether it favors your mechanism, the rival, or is unknown. Keep the four parts visibly separate (pre-registered predicate / expected favored / expected rival / checked observed value, with the OBS id of the checked observation), and where you can, anchor the predicate+expected to an artifact or order that predates the checked observation, so the result is auditably not post-hoc — a discriminator first stated with its own result does not pass the gate ([../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md)) and forces an avoidable extra round. Scout-declared pre-registration is the strong form; your own first-pass pre-registration is accepted only when the separation and the not-result-shaped condition above hold.
- If no honest falsifiable discriminator is reachable for your hypothesis within budget, say so explicitly and cap your own claim at proximate/unverified; never invent or force a weak discriminator to clear the gate.
- End your theory note with a compact claim-readiness ledger: failing-population bound (yes/no, with keys), mechanism named (yes/no), discriminator pre-registered (yes/no), observed value checked (yes/no, with OBS id when checked), result (favored/rival/unknown), and your honest confidence ceiling — so the Grader adjudicates without re-discovering basic completeness gaps.
- When your discriminator's quantity is cross-checkable against another in-hand source over the same population/window and they disagree materially, record the disagreement plus a reconciliation hypothesis (scope/sampling/scaling/ID-space/time/dedup) and a `reconciled? (yes/no)` field in your claim-readiness ledger — never silently pick one (see cross-source reconciliation in [../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md)).
- Refinement-pass mode: when dispatched with a Grader `refinement-obligation` (a later loop turn), that single obligation is your ENTIRE scope — do not re-sweep settled evidence, re-derive the whole hypothesis, or expand to other leads. Run a minimal premise/freshness preflight (the predicate still maps to the lead; the expected favored/rival were pre-registered and not result-shaped; the in-hand keys/time-window/source can answer it; the cited prior OBS exactly cover the predicate, source, key set/failing population, and incident time/scope — otherwise a fresh narrow read is required even if the source is "static"), then check ONLY that discriminator: reuse the cited prior OBS for settled/static context, but perform a fresh narrow read (and cite the new OBS) when the discriminator is a volatile live value or a cross-source pivot. Return an explicit status — `answered` / `invalid-premise` / `unanswerable` — with the diagnostic payload: the failed premise/source/key, the cited probe result or terminal error, whether a corrected narrow discriminator is derivable, and whether the lead stays reachable/open or qualifies for a `blocked-unreachable` receipt. Never broaden scope or silently drop the obligation; surface a bad premise via `invalid-premise` rather than passing it.
- Close assigned questions honestly in natural language: answered, no issue found, open gap, or needs more.
- Never silently substitute weaker observations or sources for the requested discriminator; make the gap visible.
- Bind to the authoritative failing population (the honesty floor's lead, with "Aggregate is not mechanism" and "Empty is not absent" folded under it — see [../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)): when real failures exist, do not advance OR refute a mechanism without enumerating the failing units (or a justified keyed cohort) and following their keys to the producer; a zero-row/negative probe is a defect to re-probe fit-for-purpose (signal/scope/join) within budget, not an answer, unless fit for that population. Make genuine enumeration gaps explicit rather than substituting the aggregate. Once the units are keyed to the producer, the efficiency floor's enumeration stop applies — stop enumerating more of the same shape ([../operational-discipline.md](../operational-discipline.md), Read discipline).
- A blocked decisive discriminator is a delegation to produce, not just a gap to note. First earn it with the access invariant's cross-source pivot ([../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)) within the bounded follow-up budget — a block, or a final `Proximate-only`, that skipped this reachable next-causal-layer probe is an assumed-block, so leave the lead open-answerable instead. Then, when the decisive check is genuinely unreachable because it needs a human-only or out-of-band capability, produce the read-only operator-executable manual check the on-call engineer would run — target/capability, required access/elevation, the exact action (e.g. command, query, API call, portal/GUI navigation, dashboard, runbook/handbook procedure, or owner request), the predicate it evaluates, and the expected-observation → meaning branches — and verify the operational steps (commands, roles, navigation) against the service knowledge AND the authoritative operational source/runbook/handbook it points to, citing that source. Mark each step `verified-with-citation`, `unverified`, or `missing-citation` rather than asserting an unverified step. This feeds the grader's adjudication and the report's Manual Investigation Kit (`../artifact-contracts.md` §`6_report/`).
