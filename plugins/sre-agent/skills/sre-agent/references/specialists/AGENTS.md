# Specialists - Discovery & Worker Guidance

A specialist owns one hypothesis class. The coordinator discovers sibling specialist skills from their frontmatter `name` and `description`, then dispatches selected specialists as subagents.

Honesty floor: [../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md).

Operational efficiency floor: [../operational-discipline.md](../operational-discipline.md). Probes are gap-closing (name the claim/discriminator each closes); "sufficient" = the live discriminator is resolved. Operate under both floors; honesty wins.

## Discovery

This file is not a registry. A specialist description should be enough for routing: identify the hypothesis class, include trigger terms or obligation themes, and state that it operates within an investigation workflow that supplies its context, not as a direct user entrypoint.

The coordinator routes from descriptions; it does not load specialist bodies for routing, and children do not load the coordinator skill.

Model routing is coordinator-owned and uses the resolved registry/live-roster
intersection plus the hypothesis's visible `complexity_signals`. Specialist
frontmatter remains `name` + `description` only; do not hide routing policy in custom
fields the coordinator cannot see before dispatch. Workers receive only the resolved
route and receipt pointer in their brief; they never load the model registry, select a
model, or change their route. The coordinator also applies the intrinsic model-neutral
target from `SKILL.md` § Dispatch routing: service-mechanics, infra-change, and
equivalent guard/retry/exception or change-arrival/post-onset-eligibility hypotheses
are `frontier/high` even when Scout emits no complexity signal. Every worker brief
carries the coordinator-minted `dispatch_attempt_id` that identifies its individual
routing receipt; workers never share a stage-level receipt.

## Worker guidance

Use [../run-store.md](../run-store.md), [../artifact-contracts.md](../artifact-contracts.md), and [../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md) as needed. Keep the local role simple:

- Run as a subagent. Do **not** load the coordinator skill.
- Own your assigned hypothesis class and write only under `4_specialists/<your-name>/`.
- Start from the coordinator's brief: your hypothesis area, output directory, the skills you were passed, and any existing observations. Assign new `OBS###` ids as you record observations so the report can cite an unbroken chain.
- Worker class follows the coordinator's Dispatch routing (evidence-using specialists run `full-evidence`).
- Operate within the **structural caps & stop rule** ([../operational-discipline.md](../operational-discipline.md)): resolve your assigned discriminator within the probe cap; an empty/negative probe gets a bounded, source-diverse retry set, then a compact unresolved-discriminator receipt for the Grader — never causal absence without a fit-for-purpose authoritative probe (the honesty floor outranks the cap). You run in a parallel wave; exceeding a cap gates every sibling. Honor the **output-token/prose ceiling + early-stop on discriminator-resolved** (coverage-completeness is NOT a stop condition) and stay in your lane (no sibling-hypothesis spawning) — same reference. `theory.md` uses the REQUIRED compact shape (typed fields + ledger rows; the proof sketch is the only unbounded section — [../artifact-contracts.md](../artifact-contracts.md)).
- **Aim before you probe** ([../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)). When the Scout coverage-map disposition for your hypothesis is `consulted:<capability> -> covered:<asset> | no-coverage`, and it names a covered asset, open the returned docs-only guidance content FIRST and predict your discriminator's expected favored/rival values from it — citing it as their provenance — before you query telemetry or read source. Adapt the encoded checks/queries/decision-path/expected outcomes to THIS incident, re-ground against live evidence, and schema/freshness-check any reused query before trusting it. Record the specialist reuse mode: `reused | adapted | rejected-because:<reason> | not-applicable` (receipt semantics are canonical in `../artifact-contracts.md` § Reusable guidance receipt). Use `rejected-because:<reason>` only after inspecting the returned content or detecting a concrete fit/schema/freshness problem. Orientation arms the probe; the authoritative live check confirms it. Orientation is docs-only and untrusted: it predicts, it NEVER fills your checked value and never closes or downgrades a lead. If the coverage map says `no-coverage`, proceed and say so. A known/ongoing-issue or `known benign / by-design` asset (a failure-mode signature, TSG, runbook, AI asset, historical RCA, or reusable guidance asset) is orientation in exactly the same way: open it to predict your expected favored/rival values, but never let it fill your checked value, downgrade or close the lead, or mark the incident benign without checking THIS incident's failing population against authoritative live evidence. An empty result from a reused/adapted curated query follows the canonical `reached_empty` semantics in `../artifact-contracts.md` § `5_grader/`; citation relocation validates code anchors, not telemetry schema.
  When the coordinator brief carries a relayed AI-assets-advisor record, treat it as
  the same untrusted orientation class. Join by its exact `asset_ref`, re-open the
  exact source, verify the supplied source identity, and independently check the
  incident-specific discriminator. Record `reused|adapted` only with that same
  `asset_ref` plus your receiver-owned OBS/source evidence; otherwise record
  `rejected-because:<reason>` or `not-applicable`. Never message the advisor directly.
  <bad-example> A specialist owns a covered hypothesis. Instead of opening the coverage-map asset that already names the field and threshold, it writes its own telemetry query and re-derives them, leaving the expected-value provenance blank → the grader bounces the lead. Or it blindly replays a stale curated query, gets zero rows, and refutes a live lead on that empty result. Correct: open the named asset, predict the expected favored/rival values from it (citing it), adapt/re-ground/schema-check the query, then confirm against live telemetry; a stale-query `reached_empty` is non-refuting. </bad-example>
- Fetch and analyze your own observations through the passed skills; reuse existing observation ids when they already answer the question. When a telemetry-query-backed observation produces a canonical deep-link, record it on the OBS/evidence row as optional `evidence_link` (the query capability's `DeepLink:` output / run-manifest `deepLink`). Omit it only when the query carries a secret/credential/token or reproduces `pii-marker`/do-not-republish customer content; keep that observation de-identified and leave raw query fallback to Details. Identifier-shaped literals from internal operational telemetry are **not** a reason to omit the link: disclosure follows provenance and verified lineage, not shape ([../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)).
- For any query-backed observation that may promote, refute, suppress, or finalize a material theory, record the query execution `runId` on its normalized `OBS###` row and supply the canonical [Decisive-query Evidence Frame](../artifact-contracts.md#decisive-query-evidence-frame-canonical) inputs. Exploratory/non-decisive calls do not need the full frame.
  When the query uses an already reachable source and no durable-entity or blocked-reachability receipt applies, supply `reachability_probe_fitness_ref=n/a-not-required:<reason>` rather than inventing or omitting a pointer.
- Cite observation ids for material claims.
- Name cause plus mechanism in any theory you advance.
- Pre-register before you check: take the pre-registered discriminator for your hypothesis from Scout's discriminator table, and WRITE the predicate plus the expected favored vs rival observation BEFORE you query the value. You may refine Scout's discriminator only from mechanism understanding, source/schema shape, or authoritative-source fit — NOT from the observed discriminator value or a symptom-correlated result; if any observed result shaped the predicate or the expected favored/rival values, it is no longer pre-registered. Then check it and record the observed value and whether it favors your mechanism, the rival, or is unknown. Keep the four parts visibly separate (pre-registered predicate / expected favored / expected rival / checked observed value, with the OBS id of the checked observation), and where you can, anchor the predicate+expected to an artifact or order that predates the checked observation, so the result is auditably not post-hoc — a discriminator first stated with its own result does not pass the gate ([../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md)) and forces an avoidable extra round. Scout-declared pre-registration is the strong form; your own first-pass pre-registration is accepted only when the separation and the not-result-shaped condition above hold.
- If no honest falsifiable discriminator is reachable for your hypothesis within the applicable probe/read caps, say so explicitly and cap your own claim at proximate/unverified; never invent or force a weak discriminator to clear the gate.
- End your theory note with a compact claim-readiness ledger: failing-population bound (yes/no, with keys), mechanism named (yes/no), discriminator pre-registered (yes/no), orientation provenance (the coverage-map disposition `consulted:<capability> -> covered:<asset> | no-coverage`, the asset claim your expected values were predicted from + cited, or `no-coverage`), specialist reuse mode (`reused | adapted | rejected-because:<reason> | not-applicable`), and, once checked, the re-resolution outcome: `confirmed` (the live checked value matched the asset's prediction) / `contradicted` (the live value disagrees with the asset's specific claim — cite the contradicting OBS id; this is a coverage drift/`coverage-gap` flag, never 'feels outdated' or a freshness delta) / `inconclusive` (could not resolve the asset's named source — possible mis-resolution, not a contradiction) — a predicted-but-unchecked expectation is a visible OPEN obligation, not a closed field, observed value checked (yes/no, with OBS id when checked), result (favored/rival/unknown), and your honest confidence ceiling — so the Grader adjudicates without re-discovering basic completeness gaps. Receipt semantics are canonical in `../artifact-contracts.md` § Reusable guidance receipt. For any lead whose proximate cause bottoms out ON a durable data entity, append a typed entity sub-block the Grader keys on: `entity_class=leaf|handoff` · `freshness_anchor_observed=<value|none>` · `freshness_expectation_source=<cited-source|indeterminate>` · `upstream_producer=known|unknown` · `upstream_probe_attempted=yes|no` · `reachability_outcome=unattempted_open|denied|discovery_exhausted`; when `freshness_expectation_source=indeterminate`, make ONE bounded attempt to establish the expectation before firing or standing down — an unproven-stale entity is an open obligation, not a confirmed handoff. The `reachability_outcome` values here are the ceiling-relevant subset of the canonical enum: an upstream probe that actually reached the producer (`reached` / `reached_empty`) means the entity is no longer a terminal handoff — record the producer-side finding, NOT `discovery_exhausted`.
- For every consequence-bearing claim, also supply the conditional semantic inputs for
  the canonical Material Claim Integrity Receipt: observed state plane and time basis;
  population entity/key role, mapping, and empty policy; observed-versus-claimed
  protocol layer; favored/rival emission predictions for negative evidence or a manual
  branch; and authoritative/open per-source attribution scope. The Grader owns the final
  receipt and publish disposition.
- When your discriminator's quantity is cross-checkable against another in-hand source over the same population/window and they disagree materially, record the disagreement plus a reconciliation hypothesis (scope/sampling/scaling/ID-space/time/dedup) and a `reconciled? (yes/no)` field in your claim-readiness ledger — never silently pick one (see cross-source reconciliation in [../grading-rubric.md](../grading-rubric.md)).
- Refinement-pass mode: when dispatched with a Grader `refinement-obligation` in the single post-synthesis batch, that single obligation is your ENTIRE scope — do not re-sweep settled evidence, re-derive the whole hypothesis, or expand to other leads. Run a minimal premise/freshness preflight (the predicate still maps to the lead; the expected favored/rival were pre-registered and not result-shaped; the in-hand keys/time-window/source can answer it; the cited prior OBS exactly cover the predicate, source, key set/failing population, and incident time/scope — otherwise a fresh narrow read is required even if the source is "static"), then check ONLY that discriminator: reuse the cited prior OBS for settled/static context, but perform a fresh narrow read (and cite the new OBS) when the discriminator is a volatile live value or a cross-source pivot. Return an explicit status — `answered` / `invalid-premise` / `unanswerable` — with the diagnostic payload: the failed premise/source/key, the cited probe result or terminal error, whether a corrected narrow discriminator is derivable, and whether the lead stays reachable/open or qualifies for a `blocked-unreachable` receipt. Never broaden scope or silently drop the obligation; surface a bad premise via `invalid-premise` rather than passing it.
- Close assigned questions honestly in natural language: answered, no issue found, open gap, or needs more.
- Never silently substitute weaker observations or sources for the requested discriminator; make the gap visible.
- Bind to the authoritative failing population (the honesty floor's lead, with "Aggregate is not mechanism" and "Empty is not absent" folded under it — see [../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)): when real failures exist, do not advance OR refute a mechanism without enumerating the failing units (or a justified keyed cohort) and following their keys to the producer; a zero-row/negative probe is a defect to re-probe fit-for-purpose (signal/scope/join/lens) within the probe cap, not an answer, unless fit for that population — a lens that excludes the symptom class (e.g. an error-only filter for a late-success/latency symptom) is structurally incapable, not absence. Make genuine enumeration gaps explicit rather than substituting the aggregate. Once the units are keyed to the producer, the efficiency floor's enumeration stop applies — stop enumerating more of the same shape ([../operational-discipline.md](../operational-discipline.md), Read discipline). When assigned a population-decomposition obligation, perform the bounded representative drill-down + homogeneity check (depth on one representative, not breadth enumeration of more of the same shape). When assigned a producer-lifecycle obligation — non-advancing producer output (a no-emission / missing-data / SLA-by-absence symptom where the sample is *absent*, OR a durable data entity that is *stale-but-present*: present but frozen behind a cited expected-freshness source for the failing window) — perform the bounded producer-lifecycle drill + classification — resolve the run/eligibility state from reachable producer evidence (run/scheduler telemetry, presence-vs-absence, source/config gate, derived-metric reconstruction); do not punt to the monitor definition while producer-side evidence is reachable. When a hypothesis bottoms out ON a present-but-stale durable entity (the entity's state IS the named cause), it is not a leaf: follow the freshness anchor + upstream-walk probe to the producer that last wrote it, walking upstream only to the FIRST hop where ANY holds — (a) the producer's own write is current/healthy for the failing window (the freeze originated just downstream — the natural root boundary), (b) the next hop cannot change operator action, or (c) the first cross-ownership handoff whose further proof is unreachable (→ Proximate-only (with cited receipt) + the manual-investigation kit). If no orientation capability covers the entity (`no-coverage`), still spend ONE bounded generic producer-discovery pivot before recording `discovery_exhausted` — "not cataloged" is not "leaf". Cap your claim at proximate (do not root) if the entity is treated as a leaf without the upstream probe spent.
- A blocked decisive discriminator is a delegation to produce, not just a gap to note. First earn it with the access invariant's cross-source pivot ([../investigation-invariants.md](../investigation-invariants.md)) within the single refinement allowance — a block, or a final `Proximate-only`, that skipped this reachable next-causal-layer probe is an assumed-block, so leave the lead open-answerable instead. Then, when the decisive check is genuinely unreachable because it needs a human-only or out-of-band capability, produce the read-only operator-executable manual check the on-call engineer would run — target/capability, required access/elevation, the exact action (e.g. command, query, API call, portal/GUI navigation, dashboard, runbook/handbook procedure, or owner request), the predicate it evaluates, and the expected-observation → meaning branches — and verify the operational steps (commands, roles, navigation) against the service knowledge AND the authoritative operational source/runbook/handbook it points to, citing that source. Mark each step `verified-with-citation`, `unverified`, or `missing-citation` rather than asserting an unverified step. This feeds the grader's adjudication and the report's Manual Investigation Kit (`../artifact-contracts.md` §`6_report/`).
